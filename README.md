## What I Do

I sit at the intersection of **AI** and **Security** at scale — Flipkart processes millions of transactions daily, and I'm deployed to make that infrastructure both intelligent and defensible.

Two directions. One mission:

> **AI for Security** — using ML/LLMs to make SOC, IR, and DR teams faster and more precise.  
> **Security for AI** — building guardrails so the AI we ship doesn't become the attack surface.

The gap I close every day: AppSec and GRC teams speak different languages than SOC and IR teams. I'm the bridge — translating risk into detection, policy into code, and incidents into architecture decisions. All driving toward **one unified cyber outcome**.

## How I Think About This Space

**The AI × Security stack has two layers most teams get wrong:**

1. **AI for Security** — Teams buy an AI SOC product and wonder why alert fatigue gets worse. The model isn't the problem. The signal quality is. I work on the data pipeline before the model.

2. **Security for AI** — Teams ship LLM features and treat the model as a black box. The attack surface isn't just prompt injection. It's the skill layer, the memory layer, the tool permissions. I work on the full stack.

The [OWASP Agentic Skills Top 10](https://owasp.org/www-project-agentic-skills-top-10/) and [OWASP Top 10 for Agentic Applications](https://genai.owasp.org/resource/owasp-top-10-for-agentic-applications-for-2026/) are the frameworks I anchor my threat models to right now.
